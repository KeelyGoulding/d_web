Amur Leopard

Fast Facts

  • Population: approx. 60
  • Region:Russia and China
  • Biggest Threats: Poaching and habitat loss
  • This subspecies of leopard thrives in the cold environments most leopards avoid

Description

One of the only subspecies of leopard to live in cold environments, the Amur Leopard is unique among its kin. It can grow up to 1.4 meters in length, and 48kg, although females are typically smaller. It has been reported to jump over 19 feet horizontally and 10 vertically. Although some conservation efforts have helped increase populations, this cat is still critically endangered.

Threats

Although habitat loss may become more of a concern, prey scarcity and poaching/wildlife trade are the biggest threats to the Amur Leopard. Prey scarcity means that there isn't enough food for the leopards to hunt, which doesn't allow for much predator population growth. They are also prized for their fur, and can be sold for a large price on the black market, which is why poaching could be the nail in the coffin for this species.
