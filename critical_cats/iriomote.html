Iriomote

Fast Facts

  • Population: approx. 100
  • Region: Iriomote Island, Japan
  • Iriomote Island (182 sq miles) is their only habitat
  • Biggest Threats: Habitat loss, crossbreeding

Description

This unique cat lives solely on Iriomote Island, Japan and is similar in size to a house cat. They have a coat that is a dark, dusky brown, marked with dark spots that tend to merge into bands. They look similar to a small leopard, and tend to be reclusive night dwellers. They hunt on land, in trees, and even in water to catch fish.

Threats

Habitat loss is definately the biggest threat to the Iriomote. Since it lives in such a small land mass, human settlements have greatly affected their environment. Infrastructure from these settlements (like roads) has proven to cause many accidental deaths of these cats, often from being hit by cars. In addition to human interaction, interaction with feral domestic cats is harmful to the Iriomote as well. Hybridization is the process of multiple species breeding together, which can contaminate the DNA of the original species. Basically, you end up with Iriomote/domestic cat hybrids and no more Iriomotes. Feral cats can also introduce disease to Iriomotes.
