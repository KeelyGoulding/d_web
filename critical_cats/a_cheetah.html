Asiatic Cheetah

Fast Facts

  • Population: 70-100
  • Region:Iran
  • Locally extinct in India
  • Biggest Threats:Habitat degredation, lack of prey

Description

Also known as Iranian Cheetahs, these cats used to roam over India, Pakistan, Russia, Iran, and the Middle East. Today they are limited to Iran and their numbers have dwindled. The Iranian Cheetah has a smaller head than African cheetahs, and their legs are shorter. Their coat thicker to accomodate the different environment and their neck is more powerful.

Threats

Threats to the Asiatic Cheetah include hunting, both of the cheetah and their prey. Since the prey is over hunted and scarce, the Asiatic Cheetah population cannot grow until their prey population does so. Less than 50 years ago Iran had 400-500 cheetahs, but since the late 1970s the numbers of two gazelle species fell drastically due to poaching and loss of habitat, thus reducing the cheetahsâ€™ source of food. Their habitat has also been degraded, which limits population potential, and separates subpopulations. Separation of subpopulations can lead to inbreeding or lack of breeding.
