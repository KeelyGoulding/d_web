Welcome!

Crtitically Endangered Cats is your go-to place for information on some of the most endangered wild cats, both big and small. Our goal is to raise awareness in the hope that awareness will lead to action to save these beautiful cats. All of the cats featured here have a population of less than 200 remaining, most have less than 100. The underlying cause of most of these cats' population decreases are due to human interaction or habitat loss, which is why humans need to work to help them.

What does Critically Endangered Mean?

A creature is labeled as Critically Endangered when it is considered to be facing an extremely high risk of extinction in the wild. The simplified requirements for a species to be considered critically endangered are:

  • Population reduction of 80% or more in the past 10 years or three generations
  • Inhabit severely limited or fragmented geographic ranges
  • Population of <250 and declining, or <50 mature individuals
  • Probability of extinction is at least 50% in 10 years or three generations
  • (Source: 2001 IUCN Red List Categories and Criteria version 3.1)
Simply put, critically endangered means these creatures may no longer exist in the next ten years. This is why people need to get involved! Only through dedicated action, can these critically endangered cats be saved.
