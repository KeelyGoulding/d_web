Scottish Wildcat

Fast Facts

  • Population: approx. 100
  • Region: Scottish Highlands
  • Biggest Threats: Deforestation, hunting
  • They have been known to seriously injure dogs and humans

Description

The Scottish Wildcat may look like an average house cat, but it in true Scottish fashion it has proven to be intelligent, fearless, resourceful, patient, agile and powerful; until as recently as the 1950s they were still believed to be man killers. It has well defined brown and black stripes, with a thick coat and bushy ringed tail. They are similar in size to a domestic cat, but they tend to be more muscular.

Threats

As forest dwellers, deforestation has eliminated much of their habitat and they are now mostly limited to the forests of the Scottish Highlands. Hybridization, or crossbreeding, with domestic cats has made pureblood Scottish Wildcats a rarity. They have also been hunted or killed as pests.
